not that it would/should change, this is just a fun thought experiment.

I'd make posts auto-lock or auto-sage after X number of days (determined by board, similar to sage limit) so that necroposting wouldn't happen. also get rid of the archive and thread pruning after 10 pages. instead, you could see posts from years and years ago, kind of like a more normal forum. maybe prune reply images after a year to save on disk space. I think there's a lot of valuable information on 4chan that disappears after a while. delete IP addresses after they hit autosage for sure.

I remember a discussion here about "why don't we have user registration" and there were some ideas about letting anyone make posts, but you need to have an account to upload images and/or make new threads. I think that'd be a good idea too, to combat spam and/or illegal content. I know that email addresses are free, but it adds another barrier of entry. it's not perfect, and it wouldn't be 4chan, but it might make things easier to clean up problem posters. if you have a user account, then you could have a tripcode assigned to your account and optionally (default: off) include it with your post.

how would you change 4chad? leave your ideas in the comments below, and don't forget to like and subscribe

I'm going to effortpost on this. no tl;dr.

the internet is a vastly different place than it was 20 years ago. social media was barely a concept in our brains when 4chan launched. myspace (I think) was around, facebook's public launch outside of universities was 4 years away. going on the internet to talk to people was limited to forums and chatrooms. it wasn't until like 2010 ish that 4chan even started to need the use of a captcha as the anonbb spam really ramped up. currently, 4chan has well-established methods of dealing with spam and long-established lists of proxies and tor exit nodes built up over decades of experience in dealing with these people. but if you start a new anonymous posting platform, it is going to be rife with spam and abuse. using an IPv4 address and a session cookie to track a ban is not sufficient, IMO. it is too easy to evade, especially with mobile users who can just toggle airplane mode and boop new IP address. while most people do wait out their bans, troublemakers will continue to make trouble and go out of their way to make trouble. having some sort of registration for more features like posting images or creating threads makes it easier to take care of troublemakers who have access to loads of IP addresses. I understand that it pawns off the "loads of IP addresses" issue onto "loads of email domain names", but it also gives better control over what can be posted and tracking spammers. even /biz/ has verified email addresses allowed only from a certain subset of domains. user registration is not inherently a bad thing for a site like this, and I believe that users can still remain anonymous.
